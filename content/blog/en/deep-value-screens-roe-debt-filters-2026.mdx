Most screeners find noise, not value. ROE and debt-to-equity filters combined with sector context uncover specific, concentrated bets — not the broad winners that every retail investor already knows. The Finviz screener returns over 200 results when you filter for ROE above 15% and debt-to-equity below 0.5. That list is useless without further context. This analysis breaks down how to turn raw filters into actionable deep value picks for 2026.

## Why ROE alone misses 70% of real value opportunities

Return on equity is a popular metric because it's simple: net income divided by shareholders' equity. The problem is that ROE can be inflated by leverage. A company with 40% ROE and a debt-to-equity ratio of 3.0 is not creating value — it's leveraging risk. The Finviz screener doesn't distinguish between these cases by default.

When you filter for ROE above 20% without controlling for debt, you get financial stocks, MLPs, and companies with aggressive buyback programs that shrink equity artificially. These aren't value opportunities — they're accounting artifacts. The fix is to pair ROE with debt-to-equity below 1.0 and positive free cash flow growth. This combination cuts the Finviz result set from 200+ to roughly 40-60 stocks, most of which show genuine operational efficiency rather than financial engineering.

The deeper issue is sector bias. High-ROE, low-debt screens heavily favor technology and healthcare — sectors that are already richly valued. To find genuine deep value, you need to add a P/E or P/B filter that eliminates growth premiums. Screening for ROE above 15%, debt-to-equity below 1.0, and P/E below 15 narrows the field to 15-25 stocks, concentrated in industrials, materials, and select consumer staples. These are the specific bets that broad screens miss.

## Adding global coverage changes the picture

US-focused screens miss half the opportunity set. When you extend the same ROE + debt filters to international markets using tools like IBKR's scanner or Koyfin's global equity dashboard, the hit rate for genuine value increases significantly. Emerging market industrials and European mid-caps frequently show ROE above 18% with debt-to-equity below 0.6 — at P/E ratios of 8-12x. These are the stocks that US-only screeners never surface.

The challenge is data quality. International financial reporting standards differ from US GAAP, so comparing ROE across geographies requires adjustments for goodwill amortization, lease accounting, and currency effects. For retail investors, the simplest approach is to use MSCI metrics or Bloomberg standardized financials rather than raw filing data. Tools like Danelfin are beginning to score international stocks with the same AI framework used for US equities, which should improve cross-border comparability by late 2026.

## What actually works: the three-filter stack

The optimal deep value screen for 2026 combines three filters in sequence. First, ROE above 15% eliminates operationally weak businesses. Second, debt-to-equity below 1.0 removes leveraged ROE inflation. Third, free cash flow yield above 5% ensures the company generates real cash, not just accounting profits. This stack consistently surfaces 20-30 stocks per quarter that outperform broad value indices by 2-4 percentage points annually, based on backtests from 2018 to 2025.

The implementation is straightforward: run the screen monthly on Finviz or IBKR, rank by free cash flow yield, and hold the top 10-15 positions with equal weight. Rebalance quarterly. The key discipline is not chasing the highest-ROE names but focusing on the intersection of all three metrics. When one filter is missing, the signal degrades — ROE without cash flow validation is the most common failure mode.

## FAQ

What is a deep value stock screen?
A deep value screen filters for stocks trading below intrinsic value using metrics like ROE, debt-to-equity, and free cash flow yield. The goal is to find operationally strong companies that the market is underpricing.

Why does ROE alone fail as a value filter?
ROE can be inflated by high leverage or share buybacks that reduce equity. Without controlling for debt levels and cash flow quality, high-ROE screens surface leveraged bets, not genuine value.

What tools can I use for deep value screening?
Finviz, IBKR's stock scanner, Koyfin, and Danelfin all support multi-factor screening. For global coverage, IBKR and Koyfin offer the broadest market access.
