This topic compares exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and individual stocks as investment vehicles, focusing on their structures, risk profiles, diversification benefits, and suitability for different investor goals. Understanding the trade-offs is crucial for building a balanced portfolio, as ETFs offer built-in diversification and lower risk, while individual stocks provide potential for higher returns but with greater volatility.

## What the data shows

- ETFs invest in hundreds, sometimes thousands, of individual securities (Vanguard).
- Stocks represent ownership in an individual company (Vanguard).
- Investing in ETFs can be less risky than investing in individual securities (Vanguard).
