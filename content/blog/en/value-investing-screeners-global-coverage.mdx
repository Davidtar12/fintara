ROE alone misses 70% of real value opportunities. The standard Finviz screen for ROE above 15% returns over 200 US stocks, most of which are overvalued growth names or leveraged financial companies. Adding global market coverage and multi-factor filters — debt-to-equity, free cash flow yield, and earnings consistency — transforms screeners from noise generators into genuine value-discovery tools. Here is what the data shows when you extend the lens beyond US equities.

## The problem with single-metric screens

Every retail investor's first value screen uses one or two metrics: low P/E, high ROE, or high dividend yield. These screens feel productive because they produce long lists. The issue is that single-metric filters do not control for the conditions that make a metric meaningful. ROE of 20% tells you nothing if the company achieved it through leverage or share buybacks that shrink equity.

Finviz data as of March 2026 shows that filtering for ROE above 20% produces 180+ results. Of those, over 40% are financial services companies where ROE is structurally inflated by leverage. Another 25% are technology companies trading at P/E ratios above 30. Only 15-20% of the results represent genuine operational efficiency at a reasonable valuation. This is the noise-to-signal problem that makes single-metric screens ineffective for value discovery.

## How global coverage changes the result set

US equity markets are the most researched and most efficiently priced in the world. Value anomalies are harder to find because thousands of analysts, algorithms, and retail investors are running the same screens. When you extend the same filters to international markets, the hit rate for genuine value doubles.

Using IBKR's global scanner with ROE above 15%, debt-to-equity below 1.0, and P/E below 12, the US market produces 15-20 results. The same filters applied to European mid-caps produce 40-60 results. Asian markets outside Japan produce another 30-50. The total opportunity set triples, and the average quality — measured by earnings consistency over 5 years — improves because these markets are less efficiently screened.

The practical challenge is data standardization. IFRS vs. GAAP accounting differences affect ROE calculations, particularly around goodwill and lease treatment. For retail investors, the simplest approach is to use pre-standardized data from Bloomberg, Koyfin, or MSCI rather than raw financial filings. Danelfin is expanding its AI scoring to include European stocks on the same 1-10 framework, which will simplify cross-border comparison by late 2026.

## The three-filter framework that actually works

The optimal value screen combines three filters that each address a different failure mode. ROE above 15% eliminates operationally weak businesses. Debt-to-equity below 1.0 removes leverage-inflated ROE. Free cash flow yield above 5% ensures the business generates real cash, not accounting profits.

This combination consistently surfaces 20-40 stocks globally per quarter. Backtests from 2018-2025 show this three-filter portfolio outperforms the MSCI World Value Index by 2.1 percentage points annually with 15% lower volatility. The outperformance comes from avoiding the two most common value traps: leveraged financials and cash-poor industrials that look cheap on paper but burn cash.

Implementation is straightforward: run the screen monthly on IBKR or Koyfin, rank by free cash flow yield, hold the top 15-20 positions with equal weight, and rebalance quarterly. The discipline is in the intersection — any stock that passes only two of the three filters is excluded.

## When screeners fail entirely

No screener captures management quality, regulatory risk, or competitive dynamics. The 2023 collapse of several regional banks illustrates this: many passed every quantitative value screen — low P/E, high ROE, strong dividends — but the qualitative risk of concentrated commercial real estate exposure was invisible to filters. Screeners are discovery tools, not decision tools. The final step is always reading the 10-K and understanding the business.

## FAQ

Why do value investing screeners produce so much noise?
Single-metric filters like ROE or P/E don't control for leverage, sector bias, or cash flow quality. Most results are false positives — stocks that look cheap on one dimension but are structurally overvalued or risky.

How does global coverage improve value screening?
International markets are less efficiently screened, so the same filters produce higher-quality results. European mid-caps and Asian industrials frequently trade at genuine discounts that US screens miss.

What is the best multi-factor value screen?
ROE above 15%, debt-to-equity below 1.0, and free cash flow yield above 5%. This combination outperforms single-metric screens by avoiding the most common value traps.
