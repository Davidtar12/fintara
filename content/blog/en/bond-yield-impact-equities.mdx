The relationship between bond yields and equity markets is a critical dynamic for investors, as rising bond yields make government bonds more attractive relative to stocks, often leading to capital shifts, pressure on stock prices, and changes in equity valuations. This topic matters because it influences portfolio allocation, risk premiums, and broader market stability, especially during periods of monetary policy tightening or changing economic expectations.

## What the data shows

- Rising bond yields can make government bonds more attractive compared to stocks, putting pressure on stock prices
- Higher bond yields often prompt investors to shift money from stocks to bonds
- Stock valuations may decline as bond yields rise because investors expect higher returns from stocks to compensate for increased competition from bonds
