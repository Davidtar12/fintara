Combining ROE filters with price momentum signals delivers 9.2% CAGR in 15-year backtests — outperforming both pure value (7.1% CAGR) and pure momentum (8.4% CAGR) strategies individually. The key is not using either signal alone but finding the intersection: stocks that are fundamentally cheap AND showing positive price momentum. This combination avoids the two biggest traps in value investing — catching falling knives and holding dead money.

## Why value without momentum underperforms

Pure value screens identify cheap stocks. The problem is that cheap stocks often stay cheap — or get cheaper. The classic value trap occurs when a stock with a low P/E ratio and high ROE continues to decline because the market has correctly identified deteriorating fundamentals that the backward-looking metrics don't yet reflect.

Data from the Kenneth French factor library shows that pure value portfolios (bottom quintile by P/B ratio) experienced drawdowns exceeding 40% during the 2008 crisis, the 2020 COVID crash, and the 2022 rate shock. During these periods, value stocks with negative momentum underperformed the broad market by 8-12 percentage points. The reason is straightforward: value metrics are backward-looking, while momentum captures the market's forward-looking assessment of risk.

## The momentum overlay that changes everything

Adding a simple 6-month price momentum filter to a value screen eliminates the worst performers. The methodology is straightforward: first screen for ROE above 15% and P/E below 15 (the value filter), then rank the results by 6-month price return and keep only the top half (the momentum overlay). This dual-signal approach captures stocks that are fundamentally strong AND showing positive market sentiment.

Backtests from 2010 to 2025 using this methodology show 9.2% annualized returns with a maximum drawdown of 22% — compared to 7.1% and 38% drawdown for pure value, and 8.4% and 28% drawdown for pure momentum. The improvement comes from two sources: avoiding falling-knife value traps and timing entries into recovering value stocks as momentum turns positive.

## Practical implementation for retail investors

The screen runs monthly on Finviz or IBKR. Step one: Filter for ROE above 15%, P/E below 15, and market cap above $1B (to exclude micro-caps with unreliable data). Step two: Rank the results by 6-month price return. Step three: Buy the top 15 stocks by momentum ranking. Step four: Hold for 3 months, then re-screen and rebalance.

The critical detail is the rebalancing frequency. Monthly rebalancing adds tax drag and transaction costs that eat into the CAGR advantage. Quarterly rebalancing captures most of the momentum signal while keeping turnover manageable. For taxable accounts, consider extending to 6-month holds to qualify for long-term capital gains treatment — the CAGR drops to 8.5% but the after-tax return may be higher.

## Where the signal breaks down

The value-plus-momentum combination underperforms in two specific market regimes. First, during sharp V-shaped recoveries (like March-June 2020), pure momentum strategies capture the bounce faster because they don't wait for value metrics to confirm. Second, in prolonged bull markets where growth stocks dominate, the value filter excludes the highest-returning names. During 2019-2021, this strategy lagged the S&P 500 by 3-4 percentage points annually because the filter excluded mega-cap growth stocks with high P/E ratios.

The strategy works best in normal-to-mildly-volatile markets and during transitions from bear to bull cycles. It is a risk-adjusted return strategy, not an absolute return maximizer. For investors who prioritize capital preservation alongside growth, the lower drawdown profile justifies the CAGR that slightly lags pure growth in strong bull markets.
