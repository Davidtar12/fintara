Danelfin's 1-10 AI scoring system analyses 1,200+ technical, fundamental, and sentiment features daily per stock. In 2024 backtests, portfolios built on 9-10 rated stocks returned 25.4% versus the S&P 500's 12.1%. The system works because it rank-orders probability of outperformance, not just value or momentum in isolation. But the score still misses qualitative context that retail investors need — management quality, competitive moats, and sector narrative shifts that don't show up in quantitative features.

## How the 1-10 scoring system actually works

Danelfin scores every stock from 1 to 10 based on the probability of beating the market over the next 60-90 days. The engine ingests 1,200+ features spanning three categories: technical indicators (price momentum, volume patterns, volatility metrics), fundamental data (revenue growth, margins, debt ratios), and sentiment signals (analyst revisions, earnings surprise history, news flow). Each feature is weighted by a machine learning model trained on 20 years of market data.

The key insight is that the score represents a probability ranking, not a price target. A stock scored 9 is not "better" than a stock scored 7 — it has a statistically higher probability of near-term outperformance based on the current feature set. This distinction matters because probabilities shift daily. A stock can go from 9 to 5 in a week if earnings miss or technical signals deteriorate. Retail investors who treat the score as a static recommendation miss the dynamic nature of the system.

## Where the score beats traditional screens

Traditional value screens use fixed thresholds: P/E below 15, dividend yield above 3%, ROE above 15%. These filters are useful but rigid. They cannot capture momentum shifts, sentiment reversals, or cross-factor interactions. Danelfin's advantage is that it weights factors dynamically — in a risk-off environment, defensive fundamentals get higher weight; in a momentum rally, technical signals dominate.

In 2024, Danelfin's top-decile portfolio avoided three major sector drawdowns — energy in Q1, regional banks in Q2, and utilities in Q4 — by downgrading scores before the price declines materialized. A static value screen would have held energy stocks through the entire Q1 drawdown because the fundamentals still looked strong on paper. The AI captured the deteriorating technical and sentiment signals that preceded the fundamental confirmation.

## Where the score still fails

The 1-10 system has blind spots. First, it cannot assess management quality, governance risk, or competitive positioning — factors that drive long-term returns but don't appear in quantitative features. A company with a perfect score of 10 can still collapse if the CEO departs or a patent expires. Second, the model is trained on historical data, which means it underperforms during regime changes. The 2022 inflation shock was a regime change that the model initially mispriced, though it adapted within two quarters.

Third, the score is US and Europe only. Emerging market stocks, which represent significant value opportunities, are not covered. This geographic limitation means retail investors using Danelfin as their primary screen miss half the global opportunity set. Finally, the score does not account for portfolio-level risk — it rates stocks individually, not in the context of your existing holdings. You need a separate tool for correlation analysis and position sizing.

## How to use Danelfin effectively as a retail investor

Use the score as a first filter, not a final decision. Screen for AI Score 8+, then apply your own due diligence: read the 10-K, check management tenure, assess competitive dynamics. Cross-reference with a fundamental screen on Finviz for debt levels and cash flow quality. The combination of Danelfin's probability ranking with your qualitative judgment produces better results than either approach alone.

Monitor score changes weekly. A stock dropping from 9 to 6 is a sell signal worth investigating — the model has detected deteriorating signals before they hit the price. Conversely, a stock rising from 4 to 8 may represent an emerging opportunity. The dynamic nature of the score is its greatest strength, but only if you track it actively.
