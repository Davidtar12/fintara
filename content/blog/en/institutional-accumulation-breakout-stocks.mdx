## FAQ

**What is institutional accumulation in stocks?**

Institutional accumulation is the steady, multi-quarter buying of a stock by pension funds, hedge funds, sovereign wealth funds, and endowments. It shows up as a rising share price that holds above its 200-day simple moving average while staying within a few percent of its 52-week high. The pattern reflects large pools of capital, often millions of shares, being absorbed without a sharp price spike.

**How can retail traders track institutional buying activity?**

Start with a Finviz screen requiring price above SMA200, market cap above $2 billion, and the stock trading within 3% of its 52-week high.. Cross-reference the 184-name result list against 13F-HR filings from Vanguard, BlackRock, and State Street on WhaleWisdom or SEC EDGAR. Compare quarter-over-quarter share-count changes to confirm whether positions are growing or shrinking.

**What are the best indicators for institutional volume accumulation?**

Three indicators work well together: the 200-day simple moving average as a long-term trend filter, distance from the 52-week high (within 3% is the accumulation zone), and volume confirmation on breakout days (1.5x to 2x the 30-day average is a strong signal). The 184-name Finviz screen in July 2026 combined all three into a single sortable watchlist.

**Does institutional accumulation guarantee a stock breakout?**

No. Accumulation raises the probability of a breakout but does not ensure one. A breakout requires price to clear the 52-week high on heavy volume, typically 1.5x to 2x the 30-day average. If price slides back below the 200-day SMA while still in the watchlist, the pattern has flipped to distribution and the trade is invalidated..

**How do you distinguish institutional accumulation from distribution?**

Accumulation shows price holding within 3% of the 52-week high while trading above the SMA200, with quiet, steady volume. Distribution shows price near the 52-week low or breaking below the SMA200 on rising volume. The same Finviz screen returns different tickers in each regime, which is why 13F filings and volume confirmation are necessary to confirm the signal..
