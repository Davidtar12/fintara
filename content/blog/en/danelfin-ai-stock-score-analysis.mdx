Low Risk subscore: This measures volatility and drawdown risk. AB’s 52-week range is 32.28 to 44.11, a relatively tight band. ABCB’s range is 48.27 to 87.98, much wider. ## When Danelfin’s AI Score breaks down

No scoring system works in all conditions. Danelfin’s AI Score has failure modes you should recognize.

First, the model relies on historical patterns. It’s trained on past data to predict future performance. In regime shifts—like the COVID crash or the 2022 rate-hike cycle—historical patterns may not hold. The model could lag during abrupt market transitions.

Second, the 3-month horizon is short. Danelfin’s score is designed for near-term outperformance, not long-term compounding. If you’re building a 10-year portfolio, a 3-month probability score may not align with your goals. The Best Stocks strategy’s +376% return is impressive, but it’s unclear how many trades that involved, what the turnover was, or the tax implications for a retail investor.

Third, the score doesn’t account for macro shocks. If the Fed surprises with a 50-basis-point cut, or if geopolitical events spike volatility, the model’s inputs may not react fast enough. The Sentiment subscore might capture some of this, but it’s not a real-time news engine.

Fourth, the score is a composite. A high score could be driven by one strong subscore (e.g., Technical) while others lag. You need to look under the hood. Danelfin provides subscores, but you have to interpret them. A stock with a 9/10 AI Score but a weak Fundamental subscore might be a momentum play, not a quality investment.
